Three integrated businesses

Space · Connectivity · AI.

SpaceX presents itself not as a launch company with adjacent bets, but as a vertically integrated platform whose launch capability unlocks both global connectivity (Starlink) and terrestrial-then-orbital AI compute (xAI, COLOSSUS, Terafab).

Reusable launch for commercial, civil, international, and U.S. government customers. The primary launch provider for the U.S. government. Starship — under heavy R&D — is the keystone of every downstream business.

Growth strategies

Where the proceeds — and the capital — go.

  • Space › Increase launch payload capacity; establish the lunar economy (cargo, manufacturing, energy).
  • Connectivity › Grow Starlink Broadband; expand Starlink Mobile; increase constellation capacity.
  • AI › Grow consumer AI and X monetization; deepen enterprise/gov adoption; scale terrestrial compute; deploy orbital AI; design own chips; launch digital human augmentation.
  • Future markets › Point-to-point travel, space tourism, in-orbit manufacturing, lunar & Martian transport, asteroid mining.
The repeatable model

SpaceX's seven principles.

  1. 01 · Leverage unparalleled launch capabilities to enable massive scale.
  2. 02 · Identify and create new trillion-dollar market opportunities.
  3. 03 · Design with world-class engineering and first-principles thinking.
  4. 04 · Apply The Algorithm: make less dumb, delete, optimize, accelerate, automate.
  5. 05 · Vertically integrate all the way to the end customer.
  6. 06 · Continuously drive cost down and throughput up.
  7. 07 · Generate significant cash flow and reinvest in the future.
Risk factors

What could go wrong.

The S-1 dedicates dozens of pages to risk factors. The highlights below are summarized from the prospectus and are not exhaustive. Severity tagging reflects how prominently each risk is treated relative to the company's growth strategy.

Critical · Execution

Starship dependency

The entire growth strategy hinges on Starship reaching commercial scale, full reusability, and a high launch cadence. Delays would cascade into V3 satellites, V2 Mobile, and orbital AI compute.

Critical · Regulatory

FAA launch & reentry licenses

Plans for up to one million satellites require licenses, spectrum, orbital debris approvals. Anomalies trigger investigations. Current FAA rules do not permit return-to-launch-site Starship reentries — a waiver is required.

Critical · Regulatory

FCC & international spectrum

Connectivity services depend on spectrum authorizations in each market. EchoStar spectrum deal approved by FCC May 12, 2026; closing targeted Nov 2027 but subject to conditions.

Critical · Regulatory

AI & platform regulation

Grok and X are subject to evolving privacy, content, AI-specific laws (GDPR, EU AI Act, CA Frontier AI Act). Active inquiries by Irish DPC and U.S. FTC. Spicy/Unhinged modes add reputational and legal exposure.

Critical · Financial

History of net losses

Material losses across most years. $(4.94)B in FY25; $(4.28)B in Q1 26 alone. Capex commitments imply continued cash burn ahead of orbital AI breakeven.

Critical · Governance

Controlled-company concentration

Musk will hold ~85% of voting power. Class B (10 votes/share) elects a majority of the board. SPCX will rely on Nasdaq controlled-company exemptions and not maintain a fully independent comp/nominating committee.

Material · Operational

Launch failures & space hazards

Launches and reentries carry inherent risk of failure, casualties, environmental damage. Orbital debris and collision risk grow as the constellation does.

Material · Supply

Critical components & power

Despite extreme vertical integration, certain components — notably AI processors and grid power — are sourced or constrained externally. Shortages would throttle compute build-out.

Material · Competitive

Competition intensifying

Launch, satellite broadband, and especially frontier AI all face well-funded competitors. Past outperformance is not a guarantee of future advantage.

Material · Strategic

AI segment integration

The AI segment is newly acquired and still being integrated. Strategy is evolving; significant capex required; outcomes uncertain.

Disclosed · Macro

Macro & geopolitical conditions

Global instability, sanctions exposure, currency volatility, and adverse legal regimes in international markets all flagged as risks.

Disclosed · Capital

Indebtedness & future financing

Substantial existing debt and ongoing financing needs. Future capex may require capital markets access on terms not yet known.

The offering

Terms of the IPO.

SpaceX has applied to list Class A common stock on Nasdaq and Nasdaq Texas under the symbol SPCX. Share count, price range, and proceeds remain blank in the preliminary prospectus.

Two share classes, one voice that matters.

Class A common stock entitles its holder to 1 vote per share. Class B common stock entitles its holder to 10 votes per share. Class A and Class B vote together on most matters, but Class B alone elects a majority of the board (51%, rounded up). Class B converts automatically to Class A upon most transfers.

The offering covers Class A only. After-offering Class A and Class B counts, voting percentages, and proceeds amounts remain to be filled in pending pricing.

Recent developments

The last twelve months, in order.

A series of acquisitions, partnerships, and infrastructure moves frame the company entering the IPO.

SEP 2025

EchoStar Spectrum Acquisition Agreed

SpaceX agreed to acquire AWS-3, AWS-4, and H-Block spectrum licenses from EchoStar. Amended & restated in November 2025. FCC approval granted May 12, 2026; closing targeted November 2027.

FEB 2, 2026

SpaceX Acquires xAI

xAI — founded 2023, with X Holdings folded in earlier (March 28, 2025) — becomes the AI pillar of SpaceX. Accounted for as a transaction between entities under common control; prior periods restated.

MAR 2026

Terafab Initiative Announced

Collaboration with Tesla to build a manufacturing facility targeting one terawatt per year of compute hardware. Intended to alleviate future chip shortages and reduce per-token compute cost.

APR 2026

Intel Joins Terafab

Intel — with the ability to design, fabricate, and package ultra-high-performance chips at scale — joined the Terafab project.

APR 2026

Cursor Compute & Option Agreement

Compute services + an option to acquire Cursor (Anysphere, Inc.) at a $60.0B implied equity value, paid in Class A stock. $1.5B termination fee + $8.5B deferred services fee on certain triggers.

MAY 12, 2026

FCC Approves EchoStar Spectrum Deal

Subject to remaining closing conditions, with closing targeted November 2027.

MAY 2026

Anthropic Cloud Services Agreement

Compute capacity across COLOSSUS and COLOSSUS II provided to Anthropic, PBC. $1.25B/month through May 2029, with reduced rates during the May–June 2026 ramp. Terminable by either party on 90 days' notice.
